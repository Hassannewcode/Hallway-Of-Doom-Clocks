Disclaimer & Intent

Important: The clocks presented on this site (such as the Doomsday Clock and Climate Clock) are symbolic and man-made. They are not divine prophecies, nor do they claim to predict the unseen future. They represent human assessments of global risks based on scientific, political, and ideological analyses.

This website aims to inform and educate visitors on the historical, political, and scientific significance of these clocks — not to promote belief in them as literal predictions.

Islamic Perspective on Predicting the Future

According to Islamic belief, only Allah knows the exact time of the Hour (the Day of Judgment). Human attempts to predict the end of the world are speculative and not rooted in divine revelation.

  • Qur’an 31:34: "Indeed, Allah [alone] has knowledge of the Hour."
  • Qur’an 7:187: "They ask you about the Hour—when will it come? Say: Its knowledge is only with my Lord."
  • Qur’an 33:63: "People ask you concerning the Hour. Say: Knowledge of it is only with Allah."

This website does not promote apocalyptic fear but offers insights into modern human concerns and metaphors.

JavaScript Sample (Countdown Function)

This demo snippet shows a symbolic countdown using JavaScript:


function countdown(start) {
  let seconds = start;
  setInterval(() => {
    const m = String(Math.floor(seconds / 60)).padStart(2, '0');
    const s = String(seconds % 60).padStart(2, '0');
    console.log(`${m}:${s}`);
    seconds--;
  }, 1000);
}
